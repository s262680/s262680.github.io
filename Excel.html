Cyclistic Case Study

Cyclistic, a Fictional bike share company, believes that the company's future success depends on maximising the number of annual members.The purpose of this project is to find out how annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently and understand why casual riders would choose to purchase Cyclistic annual memberships.

Dataset

This dataset was acquired from the following webpage. The data has been made available by Motivate International Inc. under this license.

Data Cleaning And Transformation



The data was cleaned in Excel, which involved the following steps:

  • Checked and removed duplicates
  • Checked and fixed data type for each columns
  • Renamed some headers and data to improve readability
  • Removed unnecessary columns that will not be used in this analysis
  • Created a new column "ride_duration" by subtracting the started_at by ended_at and converting it to time format.
  • Validated the data in this new column and removed rows that show invalid values. (E.g. some data in the started_at column are greater than the data in the ended_at column, which appears to be an error in this dataset)
  • Removed rows contain invalid data, such as inconsistent ride id length and format, blank cells, rides start or end from HQ QR station with less than few seconds short duration
  • Created a new column "day_of_week" based on the started_at date, then validated the data in a new column by comparing it to the calendar
  • Created a new column "day_of_week_text" for readability
  • Created a new column "month" for the slicer

    Analysis Results

    The analysis revealed that the majority of rides are taken by annual members, with only a small proportion coming from casual riders.

    Moreover, the data indicates that casual riders tend to use the service more frequently during weekends, while annual members show higher usage rates on weekdays.

    Additionally, the average ride duration is longer for casual riders compared to annual members.

    Conclusion

    While there is insufficient evidence to fully support this conclusion due to missing important information such as pricing plans, based on the analysis results and price plans from other bike share services (e.g., OVO Bikes), we can assume that:

    Annual members tend to utilise the service more frequently and ride shorter distances as they can use the service for free within the first 30 minutes. On the other hand, casual riders use the bike share service selectively, typically not for commuting, as indicated by their higher usage during weekends. They also tend to use this service when they need it for a long duration as they are charged for every specific period.

    The higher frequency of service use during weekdays by annual members suggests that many may be using the service for their daily commute. Therefore, promoting cycling to work could potentially encourage more riders to purchase annual memberships.

    Additionally, annual members also tend to use the service to cover short distances, which indicates that riders are more likely to cycle to work when it takes around 11 minutes on average to reach their destination. Therefore, setting up more stations near offices or popular areas could potentially increase the purchase of annual memberships.

    It is recommended to survey the annual members through the bike share app to gather more information, such as their purpose for using this service to confirm this hypothesis. Additionally, inquire about where they heard about us to identify the most optimal way to promote cycling to work once the hypothesis is confirmed.
